# Output Risk Profile

## Likely Failure Modes

- The report sounds like a theory lecture instead of giving an operational recommendation.
- Opponent reactions are asserted without payoff logic.
- Credible commitment is confused with stated intention.
- The payoff matrix hides whether numbers are observed, estimated, or assumed.
- New opponent moves are appended as notes but do not update the recommendation.
- Legal, regulatory, or antitrust-sensitive tactics are treated casually.

## Self-Repair Checks

- Put the recommendation and avoid-list before the model details.
- For every forecasted reaction, show the opponent incentive that makes it plausible.
- For every commitment, score costliness, observability, reversibility, incentive fit, and capability backing.
- Mark assumptions and estimates visibly.
- When updates exist, state whether the recommendation changed and why.
- Include a caveat when the case enters legal, financial, regulatory, or market-manipulation territory.

## Reviewer Standard

A strong report should let a CEO answer:

- what should we do now
- what will competitors likely do next
- what commitment can we make credibly
- what move should we avoid because it worsens the game
- what future signal would force a refresh
